Is there an official threshold that signifies when birthweight gets more dangerous? In other words, is there a cutoff point between a healthy birthweight and a non-healthy birthweight? Provide credible sources as necessary.

Answer

Yes, there is an official threshold that signifies when birthweight gets more dangerous.

A birth weight less than 2,500 grams is generally considered low and is used as a threshold to identify newborns who may be at higher risk for certain health complications. Low birth weight (low_bwght) infants might be at increased risk for infections, developmental delays, and other medical challenges.

The credible source is Committee to Study the Prevention of Low Birthweight; Division of Health Promotion and Disease Prevention; Institute of Medicine. Preventing Low Birthweight. for model_name, model in models.items():
    # Create a pipeline with standardization and the model to ensure all features are on the same scale
    pipeline = make_pipeline(StandardScaler(), model)

    # FITTING to the training data
    pipeline.fit(x_train, y_train)

    # Access the trained model directly as the last step of the pipeline
    trained_model = pipeline.steps[-1][1]  # steps is a list of tuples, -1 accesses the last step, 1 accesses the model

    # Feature importances or coefficients
    if hasattr(trained_model, 'feature_importances_'):
        importances = trained_model.feature_importances_
    elif hasattr(trained_model, 'coef_'):
        importances = np.abs(trained_model.coef_[0])

    # Get the top two features
    top_two_indices = np.argsort(importances)[-2:]
    top_two_features = [x_train.columns[i] for i in top_two_indices]

    # Print the top two features
    print(f"{model_name}: Top two features - {top_two_features}")

Analysis Questions 4

Which two features in your machine learning model had the largest impact on birthweight? Present one actionable insight for each of these.

Answer

Logistic Regression - Top Features: fmaps and fwhte

Actionable Insight for fmaps (Five-Minute APGAR Score): APGAR scores are used to quickly summarize the health of newborns. A low score on fmaps could indicate distress or health issues. Healthcare providers can use this information to immediately intervene, providing necessary care to improve the baby's health outcome. Regular training for medical staff on efficiently assessing and acting upon APGAR scores can enhance neonatal care.

Actionable Insight for fwhte (Father's Race: White): If race is showing as a significant factor, it may reflect underlying socioeconomic or environmental factors that correlate with birth outcomes. Public health strategies can be designed to address these disparities by ensuring equitable access to prenatal care and targeting health education programs in communities with identified needs.

Random Forest Classifier - Top Features: fage and age_interaction

Actionable Insight for fage (Father's Age): Older paternal age might be associated with increased risks for certain conditions in offspring. This insight can guide public health messaging to encourage earlier family planning or heightened prenatal screening and monitoring for older fathers.

Actionable Insight for age_interaction (Interaction term involving age): If an interaction involving age is significant, it suggests that the effect of age on birthweight is not uniform across the dataset. Identifying the specific nature of this interaction (e.g., between maternal and paternal age, or age and a health behavior) can lead to targeted interventions that address the nuanced impact of age-related factors on birth outcomes.

Decision Tree Classification - Top Features: fmaps and mage

Actionable Insight for fmaps (Five-Minute APGAR Score): Similar to the insight from Logistic Regression, emphasizing the importance of immediate postnatal care and interventions based on APGAR scores can improve neonatal health outcomes. Developing protocols to act on APGAR scores promptly can save lives and reduce the incidence of complications.

Actionable Insight for mage (Mother's Age): Young or advanced maternal age can be associated with higher risk factors for adverse birth outcomes. Public health initiatives could focus on providing extra support and resources for expectant mothers at the extremes of the age spectrum, including tailored prenatal care programs and educational resources to manage and mitigate risks associated with maternal age.
